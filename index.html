Brief History of San Jose del Monte

1750

During Spanish colonial period, reduccion (relocating settlers to the center of poblacion) was practiced in San Jose, Centro Da Baloges as part of spreading Catholicism to remote areas of Bulacan.

March 2, 1752

Foundation Day - From being a visita of Meycauayan, San Jose Del Monte was founded as a municipality. The name combines the town's patron saint and "Del Monte" (of the mountains).

January 1, 1918

The town became an independent municipality with Hon. Ciriaco Gallardo as the first Mayor through Executive Order No. 100 by Governor-General Francis Burton Harrison.

1961

Opening of the first Government Resettlement Project - Sapang Palay Resettlement Area covering 752 hectares.

September 10, 2000

SJDM was proclaimed as a Component City under Republic Act No. 8797, becoming the first city in Bulacan and the 86th city of the Philippines. Known as the "Balcony of the Metropolis".

September 19, 2017

The city achieved the World's Largest Lantern Parade in the Guinness World Records with 14,173 participants carrying hand-made lanterns made from recycled materials.

December 20, 2019

SJDM broke the Guinness World Record for "The Most Number of Living Figures in a Nativity Scene" with 2,101 participants - the city's second world record.

December 4, 2020

President Rodrigo Roa Duterte proclaimed San Jose del Monte as a Highly Urbanized City under Proclamation No. 1057 through Sangguniang Panlungsod Resolution No. 2019-059-09 after meeting minimum requirements under the Local Government Code.

July 26, 2021

RA 11896 was enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives at the 3rd Regular Session of the 18th Congress, dividing Barangay Muzon into four separate barangays.

July 1, 2022

RA 11896 lapsed into law. The plebiscite to ratify the division of Barangay Muzon was set for March 25, 2023, creating Muzon Proper, Muzon East, Muzon West, and Muzon South.

Guinness World Records

San Jose del Monte holds two Guinness World Records: World's Largest Lantern Parade (2017) and Most Living Figures in a Nativity Scene (2019).

274 Years of History

From its foundation in 1752 to becoming a component city in 2000, SJDM has grown from a small settlement to a thriving urban center with 62 barangays.
