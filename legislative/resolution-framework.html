Legislative

Resolution Framework

Resolutions passed by the Sangguniang Lungsod ng San Jose Del Monte

What is a Resolution?

A resolution is a formal expression of the opinion or will of the Sangguniang Panlungsod. Unlike ordinances, resolutions do not have the force and effect of law but serve as official statements of the legislative body.

Resolutions are commonly used for: commendations, requests to higher government agencies, expressions of support or opposition, and administrative matters of the Sangguniang Panlungsod.

How a Resolution is Passed

Step-by-step process based on RA 7160 (Local Government Code) and CSJDM rules

1

Drafting the Resolution

A Sangguniang Panlungsod member (councilor), committee, or the Mayor/City departments prepares the draft resolution. It must include: title, whereas clauses (background/justification), and resolved clauses (action items).

Legal basis: RA 7160 (Local Government Code)
2

Sponsorship / Filing with Sangguniang Secretariat

A councilor must sponsor/introduce the draft during a session or file it with the Sangguniang Secretariat so it is placed on the Calendar of Business. The secretary certifies passage/processing.

3

Referral to Appropriate Committee

On first reading, the resolution may be referred to the relevant committee(s) for review. For simple resolutions (commendations, condolences), this step may be expedited.

4

Committee Review (If Required)

For resolutions requiring technical review, committees may hold hearings or consultations. For appropriation resolutions, public hearings may be required. Citizens/affected parties may submit position papers.

5

Committee Report

If referred to committee, it issues a written recommendation (approve, approve with amendments, or disapprove) and sends the resolution back to the plenary with its report.

6

Readings in Plenary

Resolutions may be acted upon with fewer readings than ordinances, depending on local rules. Simple resolutions may be passed in one session, while more substantive resolutions may undergo:

  • First Reading: Introduction and referral
  • Second Reading: Deliberation and amendments
  • Third Reading: Final vote (if required)
7

Voting / Passage

A resolution is adopted when approved by the required majority as provided in the Sanggunian's rules (usually majority of those present). Minutes and enrolled copy are kept.

8

Certification by Sanggunian

After passage, the Sangguniang Secretary certifies the resolution. Unlike ordinances, most resolutions do not require mayoral approval unless they involve appropriations or other executive actions.

9

Transmittal (If Required)

For resolutions requesting action from higher government agencies or other entities, the certified resolution is transmitted to the addressee. For appropriation resolutions, transmittal to the Mayor follows the same 10-day rule as ordinances.

10

Filing and Archiving

The approved resolution is recorded in the Sanggunian records and copies are sent to concerned offices or addressees. Citizens can request copies from the Sanggunian or view resolutions on DILG/LGU repositories.

How Citizens Can Participate

  1. Request a councilor to sponsor a resolution on your behalf or on a matter of public interest
  2. Contact the Sangguniang Secretariat to track pending resolutions
  3. Attend committee hearings for resolutions requiring public consultation
  4. Request copies of passed resolutions from the Sanggunian
