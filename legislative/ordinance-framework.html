Ordinance Framework

City ordinances enacted by the Sangguniang Lungsod ng San Jose Del Monte

What is an Ordinance?

A city ordinance is a local law enacted by the Sangguniang Panlungsod ng San Jose Del Monte that governs the city and its residents. Ordinances have the force and effect of law within the territorial jurisdiction of the city.

Ordinances may cover various subjects including but not limited to: taxation, business regulations, public safety, environmental protection, traffic management, and zoning.

How an Ordinance Becomes Law

Step-by-step process based on RA 7160 (Local Government Code) and CSJDM rules

1

Drafting the Measure

A Sangguniang Panlungsod member (councilor), committee, or the Mayor/City departments (often with the City Legal Office) prepares the draft ordinance. It must include: proposed title, purpose/background, operative provisions, implementing agency, fiscal impact (if any), and enforcement clause.

Legal basis: RA 7160 (Local Government Code)
2

Sponsorship / Filing with Sangguniang Secretariat

A councilor must sponsor/introduce the draft during a session or file it with the Sangguniang Secretariat so it is placed on the Calendar of Business. The secretary certifies passage/processing.

3

Referral to Appropriate Committee

On first reading, the measure is referred to the relevant committee(s) (e.g., Finance, Ordinance & Legal, Zoning). Committees review, ask departments for inputs, and may hold hearings. This is the main technical review step.

4

Committee Hearing(s) and Public Consultation

Committees may hold public hearings or consultations—mandatory for certain measures (tax/levy ordinances, reclassification/zoning, transfer of public offices, major appropriations). Citizens/affected parties may submit position papers or testify.

Required by: RA 7160 IRR for specific measures
5

Committee Report

The committee issues a written recommendation (approve, approve with amendments, or disapprove) and sends the measure back to the plenary with its report and any required attachments (legal opinion, fiscal impact, summary of hearing).

6

Three Readings in Plenary

Measures undergo three readings on three separate days:

  • First Reading: Introduction and referral to committee
  • Second Reading: Deliberation and amendments
  • Third Reading: Final vote

Exceptions exist for urgent measures if certified by the Sanggunian and Local Chief Executive.

7

Voting / Passage

An ordinance is adopted when approved by the required majority as provided in the Sanggunian's rules and RA 7160 (usually majority of those present). Minutes and enrolled copy are kept.

8

Certification / Transmittal to Mayor

After passage, the Sanggunian transmits the enrolled ordinance to the City Mayor for approval. The secretary certifies passage within required timelines.

9

Mayor's Action (10-Day Rule)

Under RA 7160, the Mayor has 10 days to sign (approve) or veto (return with written objections). If the Mayor does not act within 10 days, the ordinance is deemed approved. If vetoed, the Sanggunian may override by a two-thirds vote.

Legal basis: Section 54, RA 7160
10

Posting / Publication and Effectivity

An ordinance generally takes effect 10 days after posting a copy on the City Hall bulletin board and in at least two other conspicuous places in the city. For some measures (e.g., tax ordinances), publication in a newspaper may also be required.

11

Filing and Archiving

The approved ordinance is recorded in the Sanggunian records; copies are sent to concerned offices (treasury, planning, implementers). Citizens can request copies from the Sanggunian or view ordinances on DILG/LGU repositories.

How Citizens Can Participate

  1. Identify the sponsoring councilor or committee and request the draft from the Sangguniang Secretariat
  2. Attend committee public hearings; submit a written position or evidence
  3. Track readings on the Calendar of Business; attend plenary sessions for 2nd or 3rd reading
  4. Watch the 10-day approval period and check postings for effectivity
